Composite Molds

A composite mold is a type of mold made from a combination of different materials, offering lightweight, durable, and flexible properties. It is typically made using materials such as carbon fiber, fiberglass, aramid fiber, and resins. These molds offer advantages over metal molds, including lighter weight, high strength, and heat resistance. Additionally, their flexible structure allows for the production of more complex shapes. Composite molds are commonly used in industries such as automotive, aerospace, sports equipment, and industrial manufacturing.
